20200229: Public service announcement (PSA)

Friendly reminder to:

  1. Avoid crowded places.
  2. Please wash your hand every hour for minimum of 20 seconds.
  3. Do not touch your face.
  4. When and if you sneeze, sneeze into your elbow.
  5. If you see people with symptoms remove yourself from the area, a 1m distance is recommended minimum; more if possible.
  6. Do not shake peoples hands.
  7. Avoid close contact and sexual relations with unknown persons.
  8. Disinfect your workspace and home, phone (especially phone), handles, light switches. 70% ethanol is better for disinfecting; it needs some water for disassociating and solubilising the particles.
  9. If you have elderly or ill family members you may want to consider wearing a mask for their sake, and the health of your co-workers as well. Use a filter mask, PM2.5 may work, the mechanism of its spread is still unclear.

Some facts on COVID-19 (for context comparisons in parenthesis):

  1. Enveloped viral particle (flu: enveloped).
    • Typically enveloped viral particles are more sensitive to the environment, and cannot survive without a host.
  2. RNA negative viral replication (flu: RNA positive);
    • Hopefully this will slow down mutation rate. There is one less step in the reproduction mechanism.
  3. Mortality rate 3/100 for a generalised population sample (flu: 2/1000).
    • The young and fit are not exempt.
  4. “R naught,” or R0 for COVID-19 is 3-5 (flu: 1.2 - 2, ebola: 2, measles: 12 - 18).
  5. For a close to real-time tracking of the spread of the COVID-19 outbreak please refer to this link: John Hopkins University COVID-19 Map.
    • There is some delay; currently it is estimated that there are about 200 cases in France, many in the Paris region.
    • Note that contagion seems to follow a non-linear trend (positive); hopefully not exponential. There were about 800 cases in Italy 2 days ago, now there are about 1600.

Lastly, I'd like to remind you to relax, eat well, exercise (in isolation if possible), and enjoy your families. With a little personal hygiene this situation should be containable.
